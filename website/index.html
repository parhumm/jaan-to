The Problem

AI doesn't have a consistency problem. Teams do.

The same request produces different outcomes depending on who asks, how they phrase it, and what context they remember to include.

Most people blame the AI model. The harder truth: humans are the inconsistent part.

40–60% of product work is repetitive — PRDs, specs, test plans, tracking setups, microcopy. It isn't hard work. It's work that shouldn't require thinking every time.

The Philosophy

Let AI handle the hands.
Keep humans on the mind.

AI handles
  • Formatting and boilerplate
  • Gathering context from real systems
  • Following templates consistently
  • Remembering every edge case
  • Producing structured outputs
Humans handle
  • Defining the real problem
  • Making trade-off decisions
  • Understanding customer needs
  • Exercising quality judgment
  • Knowing when to break the rules

“This is not replacing teams.
This is making teams faster.”

How It Works

Two phases. One hard stop.
Nothing ships without your approval.

Phase 1: Analysis

Read-only. No files written.

The system reads your codebase, loads team context, and asks you clarifying questions. It plans the output structure. Nothing is created yet.

Human approval required
Phase 2: Generation

Only after your explicit approval.

The system generates structured output from tested templates and saves it to a safe, sandboxed directory. Preview first. Always.

$ /jaan-to:pm-prd-write "add dark mode support"

> Loading context: tech.md, team.md
> Questions before I proceed:
  1. What problem does dark mode solve for your users?
  2. How will you measure adoption?
  3. What is explicitly NOT in scope?

> [Awaiting your answers...]
The System

Four building blocks.
Everything else is optional.

Skills

What to do

21 structured commands across 6 roles — PM, Data, Dev, UX, Core, and QA — with Growth planned. Each skill knows its questions, its template, and its output format.

Stacks

How your team works

Context files that describe your tech stack, team structure, and integrations. Written in Markdown. No schemas to learn.

Templates

Output format

Every skill produces from a tested template. Consistent structure, every time. Your team's voice, not the AI's.

Boundaries

Safety rules

Sandboxed file paths. Secret scanning. Preview-before-save. Explicit approval. Non-negotiable.
